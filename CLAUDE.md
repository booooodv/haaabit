## Design Context

### Users
Haaabit serves individual self-hosting users who open the product throughout the day to understand what must be done now, what is already complete, and whether their habit system is healthy overall. The interface needs to support fast scanning, low cognitive load, and strong trust because the same data also powers AI-assisted check-ins.

### Brand Personality
Calm, refined, reliable. The product should feel polished enough to trust with daily routines, but restrained enough to stay out of the user's way.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use `teach-impeccable` as a craft benchmark rather than a strict component kit. The product should be light-mode first, with a cool-headed and elegant visual language, strong hierarchy, deliberate whitespace, and more intentional typography than the current inline-style baseline. Avoid generic SaaS-template styling, purple-on-white defaults, noisy gamification cues, and decorative motion that competes with the work.

### Design Principles
- Make today's priorities readable at a glance before adding decoration.
- Use one coherent visual system across auth, dashboard, habits, detail, and API surfaces.
- Favor calm confidence over playful novelty or dashboard clutter.
- Let typography, spacing, and contrast establish hierarchy before gradients or ornament.
- Make responsive behavior, empty states, errors, and loading feedback feel first-class instead of bolted on.
